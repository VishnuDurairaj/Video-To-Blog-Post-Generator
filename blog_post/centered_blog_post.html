K-Means Cluster

Welcome to this detailed post on k-means cluster. Below, you will find an in-depth discussion, complete with visual examples to enhance your understanding.

Let's learn k-means algorithm in just one minute. In step 1, we have to decide how many clusters we want, which is denoted as k in k-means clustering. Let's say we are expecting 3 clusters in this case.

Then in step 2, we have to randomly initialize 3 data points. These 3 data points are called centrites.

And in step 3, we have to assign each data point to its nearest centroid. I mean take a data point, calculate distance from all three centroid and assign the data point to its nearest centroid. We have to do the same thing for each and every data point.

And in step 4, we have to update the centroid by taking mean of each group.

And in step 5, we have to repeat step 3 and 4 until the difference between previous centroid and current centroid becomes zero. Finally, this is how your cluster would look like.
